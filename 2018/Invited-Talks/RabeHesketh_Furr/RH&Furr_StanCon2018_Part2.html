Latent regression Rasch model

Model for the response of person \(j\) to item (question) \(i\):

\[ \Pr(y_{ij} = 1 | \theta_j, \delta_i) = \mathrm{logit}^{-1} ( \theta_j - \delta_i ) \]

\[ \theta_j | \mathbf{x}_j', \boldsymbol{\lambda} \sim \mathrm{N}(\mathbf{x}_j' \boldsymbol{\lambda}, \psi^2) \]

  • \(y_{ij} = 1\) if the response is correct, \(y_{ij} = 0\) otherwise
  • \(\theta_j\) is the ability for person \(j\)
  • \(\delta_i\) is the difficulty for item \(i\)
  • \(\boldsymbol{\lambda}\) is a vector of regression coefficients
  • \(\mathbf{x}_j\) is a vector of person-related variables

More on IRT and Stan: https://education-stan.github.io/

Reparameterizing the model

Model may be parameterized in terms of residual \(\zeta_j\):

\[ \Pr(y_{ij} = 1 | \mathbf{x}_j, \boldsymbol{\lambda}, \delta_i, \zeta_j) = \mathrm{logit}^{-1} ( \mathbf{x}'_j \boldsymbol{\lambda} + \zeta_j - \delta_i ) \]

\[ \zeta_j \sim \mathrm{N}(0, \psi^2) \]

Non-hierarchical priors are:

\[ \lambda \sim t_3(0,1) \]

\[ \psi \sim \mathrm{exp(.1)} \]

\[ \delta_i \sim t_3(0,3) \]

Conditional and marginal likelihoods

Conditional likelihood for \(y_{ij}\) at posterior draw \(s\):

\[ f_\text{c}(y_{ij} | \mathbf{x}_j, \boldsymbol{\lambda}^s, \delta_i^s, \zeta_j^s) = \Pr(y_{ij} | \mathbf{x}_j, \boldsymbol{\lambda}^s, \delta_i^s, \zeta_j^s) \]

Marginal likelihood for \(\mathbf{y}_j\) at posterior draw \(s\):

\[ f_\text{m}(\mathbf{y}_{j} | \mathbf{x}_j, \boldsymbol{\lambda}^s, \delta_i^s, \psi^s) = \int \prod_{i=1}^I f_\text{c}(y_{ij} | \mathbf{x}_j, \boldsymbol{\lambda}^s, \delta_i^s, \zeta_j) p(\zeta_j | \psi^s) ~ d\zeta_j \]

Adaptive quadrature

Let \(\tilde \mu_j\) and \(\tilde \phi_j\) be the posterior mean and standard deviation for residual \(\zeta_j\)

Let \(a_{m}\) and \(w_m\) be the location and weight for standard quadrature node \(m\) (from statmod package, for example), for \(m = 1 \ldots M\)

Adaptive quadrature node locations and weights for cluster \(j\) are:

\[ a_{jm} = \tilde \mu_j + \tilde \phi_j \times a_m \]

\[ w_{jm}^s = \sqrt{2\pi} \times \tilde \phi_j \times \exp \left ( \frac{a_{jm}^2}{2} \right ) \times g \left ( a_{jm}; 0, \psi^{2,s} \right ) \times w_m \]

Marginal likelihood is then approximated as:

\[ f_\text{m}(\mathbf{y}_j | \mathbf{x}_j, \boldsymbol{\lambda}^s, \delta_i^s, \psi_j^s)\ \approx \ \sum_{m=1}^M w_{jm}^s \prod_{i=1}^I f_\text{c}(y_{ij} | \mathbf{x}_j, \boldsymbol{\lambda}^s, \delta_i^s, \zeta_j^s=a_{jm}) \]

An extension of Rabe-Hesketh, Skrondal, & Pickles (2005)

Example dataset: Verbal agression (1/2)

24 items, for example:

  • "A bus fails to stop for me. I would want to curse."
  • "I miss a train because a clerk gave me faulty information. I would scold."
  • "The grocery store closes just as I am about to enter. I would shout."

Responses are coded:

  • 1 = "Perhaps" or "Yes"
  • 0 = "No"

From Vansteelandt, K. (2000). Formal models for contextualized personality psychology. Unpublished doctoral dissertation. K. U. Leuven, Belgium.

Example dataset: Verbal agression (2/2)

The data consists of:

  • \(I = 24\) items
  • \(J = 316\) persons

  • Two person covariates (anger, male):

    1. male, whether the person is male (.5) or female (-.5)
    2. anger, a score from a separate instrument on trait anger (mean = 0, SD = .5)

Model variants

Five latent regression Rasch models are fit to the data, differing in the person covariates included:

  1. none
  2. anger only
  3. male only
  4. anger and male
  5. anger, male, and their interaction

10,000 posterior draws (after warm up) are obtained for each

11 adaptive quadrature nodes are used for marginal likelihoods

Replicate 10 times to indicate Monte Carlo error
